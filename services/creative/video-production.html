What It Is

Video as a performance asset.

Most video work gets produced for awards reels and ends up invisible in the feed. We produce video for the actual job — the scroll-stopping hook, the case study that closes the deal, the 15-second ad that converts, the keynote that lands.

Revelation runs three production tracks — traditional cinematography, AI-assisted, and hybrid — and picks the one that fits the channel, the budget, and the speed required. Not the one that's trendy.

"Video that has a job."

Built for the Channel

Ads, social, website, keynote, long-form — each format gets its own edit, pacing, and cut. Not one master video shoehorned into six places.

Traditional Production Muscle

Cinema cameras, lighting, direction, sound, color, editing, VFX — full cinematic production when the project earns it.

AI-Native When It Wins

AI-generated shorts, avatar spokespeople, synthesized footage — used where they genuinely outperform traditional production on speed, cost, or iteration.

Hybrid When It's Smarter

Live-action hero shots + AI-generated cutaways, AI upscales, stylistic treatments, or scaled-out variations. The track that wins is whichever moves the result.

Three Production Tracks

Pick the track. Or let the project pick it.

Every video project starts by deciding which production track fits the outcome, the channel, and the turnaround — then we build around that decision.

Traditional

Cinematic production. Cameras, crew, lighting, direction, color, edit. Built when the project needs cinematic credibility — brand films, high-stakes case studies, broadcast-ready ads.

AI-Native

AI-generated scenes, avatars, and shorts. Built when the project needs speed, volume, or iteration — campaign variations, AI spokespeople, rapid-test creative, scaled content.

Hybrid

The best of both. Live-action hero + AI enhancement. Most of the work we do lives here — cinematic credibility where it matters, AI leverage where it scales.

Who It's For

For operators whose video doesn't move anything.

Video engagements are best for businesses whose current video either doesn't exist, doesn't convert, or doesn't scale — and who are ready to treat video as a performance asset instead of a branding flourish.

  • Brands running ads without a video — or with one video the platform is already fatigued on
  • Founders who need a keynote, explainer, or case study to close enterprise buyers
  • Operators who want to test 20 ad variations this quarter instead of producing one
  • Teams with a product that's hard to explain without seeing it in motion
  • Companies ready to scale content output using AI without losing the production floor
